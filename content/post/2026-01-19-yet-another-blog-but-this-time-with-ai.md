Truth be told this blog does not have AI integrated into. I used AI rewrite and remove a custom blog engine and relaunched (yet again).

*Say what now?*

The story of this blog is one of experimentation. I have always used it as my personal testing ground for various ideas I have been mulling over. As they say, every developer either writes their own blog engine or is thinking about it.

##  The MVP HTML Launch (2021)

The original blog format was a git repository with static, hand coded HTML. It was hosted on GitHub Pages. My goal was to launch and not spend too much time fiddling with blog engines and themes. I found a Mardown stylesheet and got it going. This was in [September of 2021][post/0-zero-based-blog] and the theme stuck.

## The Great Jekyll Conversion (2022)

Next I moved to [Jekyll][JKL] after getting tired of copy+pasting HTML snippets. (Dec 2021)

## The Great Blogstatic Migration (2023)

- I had a good run with [Jekyll][JKL] but eventually migrated to Blogstatic which became [BlogMaker][BST] (Jan 2023)

## Evolution of my Twitter Game (2024)

I missed the entire year of 2024 and spent too much time on Twitter/X.

## The Greatest Migration Ever (2025)

- 2025 was light year with minial posts. By then the app rean on a custom ASP.NET Core framework.

## The AI MVP Markdown Launch (2026)

So now I am starting with AI in 2026 to bring it full circle. No more database. I am editing static [Markdown][MKD] files using [Typora][TYP] (just the way that I wanted it). This is still an [ASP.NET Core][ASP] application that runs at a host. Digital Ocean to be exact.
The goal was to use GitHub Copilot to remove the backing database and external tooling. Given a few prompts that was accomplished rather easily.
